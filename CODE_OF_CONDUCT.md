# Code of Conduct

## The short version

Be decent. Assume the other person is trying.

## What that means here

**Good faith is the default.** Someone filing a confused bug report is not wasting your time — they
are telling you the documentation failed. Someone disagreeing with a design decision is not attacking
you. Read the strongest version of what they said, not the weakest.

**Criticise the work, not the person.** "This function can lose secrets if the disk fills up" is
useful. "You clearly don't understand encryption" is not, and it is usually wrong.

**Nobody owes you an answer.** This is a project people work on in their own time. A question that
goes unanswered for a week has not been ignored — it has been queued.

**Say what you actually mean.** Sarcasm, passive aggression and "well, obviously…" all read worse in
text than they sound in your head, and they read worse still to someone whose first language is not
yours.

## Not acceptable

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks — in issues, pull requests, discussions, or anywhere else
  connected to this project.
- Demeaning anyone for their experience level, background, identity, or language.
- Posting someone's private information.
- Publishing a security hole before it is fixed, in order to score a point. Report it
  [privately](https://github.com/BEKO2210/Knowledge-Hub/security/advisories/new).
- Deliberately wasting people's time: spam, trolling, bad-faith arguments.

## If something goes wrong

Report it to **belkis.aslani@gmail.com**. Reports stay confidential, and the person reporting will
not be exposed for having reported.

Responses scale with the behaviour, and they are the maintainer's call:

1. A private word, explaining what was wrong with it.
2. A public correction, and the comment removed.
3. A temporary ban from interacting with the project.
4. A permanent one.

Reasonable people will never reach step 2.

## Scope

This applies anywhere the project lives: issues, pull requests, discussions, the wiki, and any space
where you are representing Knowledge Hub.
